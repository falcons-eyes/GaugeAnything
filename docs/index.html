Detection ≠ Measurement

Finding It Is Easy. Reading It in Millimetres Is the Hard Part.

Anyone can put a box on the page. The research problem is recovering a physical quantity — mm, μm — under perspective, motion, and scale ambiguity.

Same detected page, two ways to read its size. Naive "detect + track" (apparent pixels × fixed scale) swings to 27% median, 44% peak error as the camera moves; GaugeAnything's per-frame metric homography holds 1.5% — and outputs a rectified document, not just a box. Detection was never the hard part.

Metric survives motion. TUM handheld RGB-D — mm from depth while the camera moves. 66/70 measurable, 0.98%; bad frames rejected, never guessed.

A box can't give you 0.23 mm. The crack is a brightness valley, ~50 px in a 9448 px scan; width read sub-pixel from the profile. Synthetic Ken Burns over a static krkCMd scan.

Abstract

Promptable foundation models — Segment Anything, Depth Anything, Count Anything — generalized the perception of industrial inspection, yet the metrology that drives field decisions (crack width, part dimension, condition grade) remains absent. We present GaugeAnything: from an image and a prompt, it emits instances, counts, metric dimensions (mm ± σ), and condition grades as atomic outputs, by pairing a frozen SAM 3 backbone with a metrology core. Our central finding — mask for where, signal for how wide — reaches 23.2 μm median physical crack-width error and 2.5% promptable part dimensions on CAD ground truth, where reading width from mask geometry costs 4–6×. Everything is audited, every negative reported, and the full benchmark, models, and a live inference server are released.

Rigor

We Attacked Our Own Results First

Most reported numbers are inflated by leakage or confounds. We surface the traps — and the negatives — before a reviewer does.

Measurement gap
Segmentation metrics hide it

SAM 3 wins mIoU yet its 62.9% relative width error is worse than classical adaptive (43.5%). Best mask ≠ best measurement.

Honest negatives
We publish what fails

Zero-shot dense counting collapses; a matting head that won 20× on synthetic data failed to transfer until the synthesis was made realistic. Reported, not hidden.

Calibrated uncertainty
It says "not measurable"

Per-source conformal intervals keep 90% coverage; gated frames are reported as not measurable rather than guessed — the instrument's contract.
